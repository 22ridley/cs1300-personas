Case Study

PERSONAS

This case study examines a public interface through the lens of users with different experiences, needs, and results. The goal is to learn the perspectives of these users through interviews and observations, and ultimately create personas to understand how this interface does or does not meet the needs of these users.

Preparation

The interface I chose for this Case Study is the laundry machines in the dorm buildings at Brown University. The skech above displays one such machine, as well as annotations describing how users interact with this interface. As shown, this interface is comprised of a touch screen as well as washers and dryers themselves. The touch screen, which is physically separate from the washers and dryers, is where users select machines and pay. The users press `Start` on the machines themselves after paying on the touch screen.

Questions

After creating my sketch of ths interace, I then considered the questions I needed to ask users to understand how they use the interface and whether it meets their needs. Below is the list of questions, and if you click on one, you can read about what I hope to gain by asking each question.

A. What is your class year at Brown?

This question indirectly gains an understanding of how long the student has been using these laundry machines. Asking a student "About how many times have you used the laundry machines at Brown?" could lead to overestimation, in order to avoid appearing as if they don't wash their clothes enough.
